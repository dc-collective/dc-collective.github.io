ISSUE ONE

April 1, 2020 by BatJamags

PANACEA PART ONE: THE MIRACLE CURE

PAGE ONE – ONE PANEL

Panel One
It’s night. We see the Gotham skyline from above. The Bat-Signal is painted across a cloud drifting past the reader.

  1. TITLE: PANACEA PART I – THE MIRACLE CURE

PAGE TWO – NINE PANELS

Panel One
Three men in dark clothes and ski masks are gathered around a rooftop door. One is kneeling, trying to pry the door open with a crowbar. We can still see the Signal in the background.

  1. BATMAN: [Off-panel] I assume you lost your keys, gentlemen?

Panel Two
The three burglars look over their shoulders in shock. A dark shadow is cast over them. The edge of BATMAN’s cape is blowing into the foreground.

  1. BURGLAR 1: B-B-B-
  2. BURGLAR 2: Don’t stutter at him…

Panel Three
Closeup of BURGLAR 2.

  1. BURGLAR 2: [Yelling] Get him!

Panel Four
Low angle of Batman, in silhouette, backhanding Burglar 2 as the other two burglars approach him.

Panel Five
Closeup on Batman’s hand, grabbing BURGLAR 3’s crowbar and jabbing it into his stomach.

Panel Six
BURGLAR 1 is now holding a pistol, pointing it at Batman.

Panel Seven
Closeup of Batman’s face, twisted into a snarl.

Panel Eight
Burglar 1 drops the gun and raises his hands in surrender.

Panel Nine
The three burglars are tied up together, lying next to the still-lit Bat-Signal.

  1. GORDON: [Off-panel] I’m getting too old for this.

PAGE THREE – SEVEN PANELS

Panel One
COMMISSIONER JAMES GORDON is standing in the foreground, lit by the signal off to the side. Batman is on the edge of the roof in the background, still in silhouette.

  1. BATMAN: We all are, Jim.
  2. GORDON: Hmph. And here I was starting to buy the rumors that you’re immortal.
  3. BATMAN: [Caption] James Gordon. Police Commissioner. My friend.

Panel Two
Closeup of Batman stepping into the light. The expression on his face doesn’t quite rise to the level of a smile, but is at least more pleasantly neutral than usual.

  1. BATMAN: No such luck. Why the Signal? Did you locate any of the Arkham escapees*?
  2. CAPTION: *A mass breakout happened in the smash DCW: Alpha oneshot! Where were you? - SelfEditingJamags

Panel Three
Closeup of Jim lighting his pipe.

  1. GORDON: Don’t count yourself out just yet. Call came in from the CEO of Gothcorp , of all people.

Panel Four
Low angle of Batman and Jim.

  1. BATMAN: The new one. Gorman?
  2. GORDON: The same. He tells me Gothcorp is rolling out some kind of miracle drug. He was cagey with the details, but to hear him talk you’d think it turns lead into gold and donkeys into unicorns.
  3. BATMAN: Smells like a con.
  4. GORDON: Probably is . But Gorman seems to think that after the public announcement, someone will try to steal it. That’s where you come in.

Panel Five
Closeup of Batman, one eyebrow raised.

  1. BATMAN: So I’m playing watchdog for a phony drug? Now, of all times?

Panel Six
Jim turns to gesture at a skyscraper in the distance with “Gothcorp” written across the side in large neon letters.

  1. GORDON: Thought you should at least hear about it. I’m more worried about what happens if one of our escapees hits their headquarters. Besides, if it is all a fraud, you can—

Panel Seven
Jim turns back towards Batman and he’s gone.

  1. GORDON: —Every damn time.

PAGE FOUR – THREE PANELS

Panel One
Low angle on the Batmobile driving down a Gotham street, splashing water up towards the reader. Dawn is beginning to peek through the clouds in the background.

  1. BATMAN: [Cap] I’ve been watching Gorman ever since I helped land his predecessor in prison.
  2. BATMAN: [Cap] He’s invited Bruce Wayne—and about half the reporters and socialites in Gotham—to a reception at Gothcorp International Headquarters in the Diamond District of Old Gotham. He’s set to make a major announcement.
  3. BATMAN: [Cap] Unless I miss my guess, Jim’s just spoiled the big surprise for me.

Panel Two
Show the Batmobile pulling into the cave’s entrance from inside, a red-and-white striped roadblock rising back into place in the background outside.

Panel Three
Batman is exiting the Batmobile. ALFRED is dusting the Bat-Computer in the background.

  1. ALFRED: I trust tonight’s adventures were suitably violent, Master Bruce?
  2. BATMAN: [Cap] Alfred Pennyworth. Family Butler. He raised me after my parents’ death.

PAGE FIVE – FIVE PANELS

Panel One
Closeup on Batman removing his mask to reveal BRUCE WAYNE.

  1. BRUCE: Just a few second-story men. No trouble.
  2. ALFRED: [Off-panel] My condolences, sir.

Panel Two
Alfred folds his arms and gives his best disapproving stare.

  1. ALFRED: And might I ask what urgent matter brings you to your ancestral home at five in the morning?

Panel Three
Bruce is hanging up his cape and belt.

  1. BRUCE: Actually, I thought I might catch up on some sleep so I can attend a social function.

Panel Four
Closeup on Alfred, one eyebrow raised skeptically.

Panel Five
The same angle.

  1. ALFRED: Are you certain you’re entirely all right, sir?

PAGE SIX – ONE PANEL

Panel One
Establishing shot of a municipal-looking building, with copious columns and relief sculptures. It’s daytime. A small figure is ascending its front steps.

PAGE SEVEN – FIVE PANELS

Panel One
Shot over the shoulder of DEREK ALTMAN, a balding, overweight man in a suit, looking down the steps at CHELSEA MCKAY, a twenty-something woman, about four feet tall, in an overcoat with round, monolithically large glasses. A resemblance to Velma Dinkley would not be entirely coincidental.

  1. CHELSEA: Sorry to keep you waiting. Uh, am I late?
  2. DEREK: Just on time. And it’s not exactly an appellate proceeding.

Panel Two
Chelsea and Derek are filing through the door into a large, marble-tiled lobby.

  1. CHELSEA: I just wanted to thank you again. For taking me on, that is.
  2. DEREK: Thank us? With your credentials?

Panel Three
Closeup of Chelsea.

  1. CHELSEA: Well, I mean, it’s not- It’s just-

Panel Four
Closeup of Derek.

  1. DEREK: Don’t be so modest, kid. Tell me, why’d you give up clerking?

Panel Five
The two pass through another door, which swings out towards the reader so that the writing on the frosted glass is legible. It says “DISTRICT ATTORNEY’S OFFICE.”

  1. CHELSEA: I think you know why.

PAGE EIGHT – EIGHT PANELS

Panel One
Shot of the doorway from inside the office. The office well lit, but cluttered with cardboard boxes full of paperwork. Several desks are scattered around the room. Derek has turned to face Chelsea.

  1. DEREK: Your old man was a helluva lawyer. I always said they don’t make ‘em like Chuck McKay anymore, but I figure you’ve got plenty of time to prove an old fart like me wrong.
  2. VIC: [Off-panel] She just got here, Altman. Let her get a conviction or two before we crown the next Harvey Dent.

Panel Two
VIC CLARENCE, a scruffy-looking man in his mid-thirties, shakes Chelsea’s hand from his desk while Derek folds his arms in the background.

  1. CHELSEA: Chelsea McKay, I’m the new ADA.
  2. VIC: Vic Clarence. I’m an old ADA.
  3. DEREK: You know better than to joke about Harvey, Vic.

Panel Three
Vic waves his hand at Derek.

  1. VIC: C’mon, Derek. Arkham screwballs are a fact of life for us.

Panel Four
Closeup of Derek, grimacing.

  1. DEREK: Harvey’s different .

Panel Five
JANET CROFT, a middle-aged woman in a crisp suit, leans out from behind another desk.

  1. JANET: Easy, boys. New kid in town doesn’t need to see you two tearing each other’s throats out. Want me to get her set up with a caseload?

Panel Six
Derek straightens out his suit as Chelsea walks over to Janet.

  1. DEREK: Yes, of course. Thank you, Janet.

Panel Seven
Janet lays out a stack of papers on the desk in front of Chelsea.

  1. JANET: Welcome to the family, McKay. One good thing about working in Gotham is you never get bored.
  2. CHELSEA: I don’t suppose any of this relates to… the Penguin?
  3. JANET: Trust me, kid.

Panel Eight
Closeup of Janet.

  1. That’s one case you’d better hope you never get.

PAGE NINE – EIGHT PANELS

Panel One
High angle of a police car pulling up to the curb next to an old, worn-down brownstone. It’s darker than the last scene, but still day. Two more police cars are already parked.

Panel Two
HARVEY BULLOCK exits the cruiser holding a half-eaten doughnut. OFFICER 1, a uniformed officer, is stepping up to greet him.

  1. BULLOCK: OK, where’s the stiff?
  2. OFFICER 1: Uh, it’s upstai-
  3. BULLOCK: Just show me. And while we’re on the way, tell me why Major Crimes got this instead of Homicide, huh?

Panel Three
Angle from behind Bullock and Officer 1 as they’re walking down a hallway. There are doors, some of which have numbers and most of which have places where numbers used to be before they were worn off over time. The carpet has probably not been vacuumed in twenty years. OFFICER 2 is ducking into a door ahead.

  1. BULLOCK: That the place?
  2. OFFICER 1: That’s the bathroom, Detective Bullock.

Panel Four
Angle from ahead of Bullock and Officer 1 as they pass the bathroom door. Bullock’s doughnut is still in his hand, albeit a few bites smaller.

  1. OFFICER 2: [From inside the bathroom] Urrrrf!
  2. BULLOCK: Rookies.

Panel Five
Officer 1 points into an open door.

  1. OFFICER 1: In here, sir.

Panel Six
Closeup of Bullock in the doorway.

Panel Seven
The same angle. Bullock is holding up his doughnut and looking at it suspiciously.

Panel Eight
Bullock turns back to Officer 1.

  1. BULLOCK: I wasn’t gonna eat this anyway. You want it?

PAGE TEN – THREE PANELS

Panel One
Bullock and Officer 1 are leaning on either side of the bathroom door. Inside, Officer 2 is washing his face.

  1. BULLOCK: So, this mess has been here, what? A day? Two days?
  2. OFFICER 1: Or longer.
  3. BULLOCK: Let me guess. The landlord only found it because the neighbors complained about the smell.
  4. OFFICER 1: Apparently, one of them threatened to call a lawyer.

Panel Two
Officer 2 steps up to the doorway.

  1. BULLOCK: So our perp’s long gone, and I ain’t certain , but that didn’t look like enough chunks to make a whole human, so that ain’t even all of our victim .

Panel Three
Officer 2 is now kneeling back by the toilet.

PAGE ELEVEN – THREE PANELS

Panel One
Bruce is riding in the back of an expensive-looking black sedan. It’s night again outside. We see the edge of Alfred’s shoulder in the driver’s seat.

  1. BRUCE: Is everyone in position?
  2. ROBIN: [Via communicator] I’m set up with visual, but the most suspicious thing I’ve spotted is whatever they’ve got on the hors d’oeuvres tray.
  3. BATMAN: [Cap] Robin. An apprentice of sorts. The third to hold the title.
  4. ORACLE: [Comm] I’m inside their system. Their security is cutting edge… for about fifteen years ago.
  5. BATMAN: [Cap] Oracle. Formerly known as Batgirl. Now the superhero world’s foremost researcher and tech expert.

Panel Two
Closeup of Bruce through the side window.

  1. BRUCE: Any data on our miracle drug?
  2. ORACLE: [Comm] Weirdly, no. Almost nothing. I’ve got some references to a “Project: Panacea” in budget documents but no testing records, no advertising material, and certainly no formula.
  3. BRUCE: Keep me posted. All right, I should be fashionably late enough by now. You can stop circling, Alfred.

Panel Three
Wide shot of Bruce exiting the car onto a crowded sidewalk full of well-dressed people.

  1. VICKI: [Off-panel] Bruce!

PAGE TWELVE – FOUR PANELS

Panel One
Bruce turns, facing VICKI VALE, who is running up to him waving a notepad.

  1. VICKI: I wasn’t expecting to see you here. Any insight on the mystery announcement?
  2. BRUCE: I wasn’t expecting to see me here either. I’m just a curious citizen tonight.
  3. BATMAN: [Cap] Vicki Vale. Reporter for the Gotham Gazette. We were close once. I made some sacrifices and more mistakes, but she eventually forgave me.

Panel Two
Shot from in front of the two as they shuffle through the front door into a huge, ostentatious glass atrium.

  1. VICKI: You sure you’re not holding out on me?
  2. BRUCE: Promise. I’ll probably learn more from whatever you write than I will from the announcement.
  3. VICKI: Oh, I’ve heard that one before, Mr. Wayne.

Panel Three
Shot over Bruce’s shoulder as Vicki flits off into the crowd.

  1. GUS: [Off-panel] Ladies and gentlemen!

Panel Four
Low angle of GUS GORMAN, a man in his thirties who fills out a suit very nicely, standing at a lectern at the rear of the atrium. A well-dressed elderly couple are exiting an elevator, a janitor is entering another, a woman in a black dress is going into the stairwell, and a flustered-looking waitress is trying to slide across the edge of the stage to get back into the crowd.

  1. GORMAN: I’m so glad you all could make it, and I hope you like… whatever it is the hors d’oeuvres are.
  2. BATMAN: [Cap] Gus Gorman. Wall Street turnaround specialist. Brought on as CEO to salvage Gothcorp’s stock price after Ferris Boyle went to prison for insider trading.

PAGE THIRTEEN – SIX PANELS

Panel One
Same angle, but pull in a little closer on Gorman.

  1. GORMAN: It won’t do any of us any good to sugarcoat things. Gothcorp has made a lot of mistakes in the past. But I was hired to change all that. I was hired because I believe in change, progress, and new beginnings.

Panel Two
Move in for a closeup. Gorman is reaching into his jacket.

  1. GORMAN: Our incredible research team has been moving heaven and earth to create our new beginning. And the fruit of that effort…

Panel Three
Extreme closeup of Gorman’s hand holding a glass vial full of an almost-clear liquid with a very slight green tint.

  1. GORMAN: … Is this .

Panel Four
Back out to the closeup. Gorman is holding the vial in front of his right eye.

  1. GORMAN: We call it “Panacea,” like the ancient Greek goddess of health— universal health, apparently. And you aren’t gonna believe this, but I’ll be darned if that isn’t what it does. Our testing so far indicates that Panacea can, with sufficient time and dosage, heal any injury.

Panel Six
The crowd erupts into raised hands and shouted questions. Bruce, in the foreground, is glancing suspiciously to the side.

  1. CROWD 1: How does it work?
  2. CROWD 2: Isn’t this too good to be true?
  3. CROWD 3: You don’t expect us to believe this?
  4. BRUCE: Something’s wrong about this.
  5. ORACLE: [Comm] Everything’s wrong about this.
  6. ROBIN: [Comm] But what if it really does do what he says?
  7. ORACLE: [Comm] It doesn’t.

PAGE FOURTEEN – SIX PANELS

Panel One
A heavyset man in the crowd trips and falls.

  1. FALLING MAN: [SFX: Ghugh]
  2. CROWD 4: Oh my God!

Panel Two
Bruce turns to look at the fallen man as a few more people begin to drop.

  1. BRUCE: Robin. Notice anything off about the waiters?
  2. ROBIN: [Comm] No, wh- Wait. There are none.
  3. BRUCE: Not now . So where…?

Panel Three
Low angle of a woman with long, dark hair in a waitstaff uniform approaching Gorman. She’s withdrawing a dagger from her sleeve.

  1. ROBIN: [Comm] There! Along the sides!

Panel Four
Bruce tumbles over into a small round table covered with a tablecloth.

  1. BATMAN: [Cap] This won’t be my cleanest change, but it’ll have to do.

Panel Five
We see the tablecloth lying overtop of Bruce.

Panel Six
Batman rises from the floor towards the reader, throwing the cloth off.

PAGE FIFTEEN – FIVE PANELS

Panel One
The waitress assassin lunges at Gorman, knife in hand.

  1. GORMAN: Someone, help!

Panel Two
Extreme closeup of a Batarang slicing through the assassin’s hand, making her drop the knife.

  1. SFX: SKKT!

Panel Three
Pull out as Batman follows his weapon, sending the assassin sprawling with a punch to the face.

  1. GORMAN: You did come!

Panel Four
Batman grabs Gorman by the shoulder and begins to drag him away.

  1. BATMAN: There are more of them! Move!

Panel Five
Another waitress, this one with shoulder-length blonde hair, is kneeling and firing a pistol.

  1. SFX: BLAM! BLAM! BLAM!

PAGE SIXTEEN – SIX PANELS

Panel One
Batman has his cape draped over Gorman and is pulling him into the stairwell. The gunshots are barely missing them.

  1. SFX: VTT! VTT! VTT!

Panel Two
A third waitress assassin, also blonde but with shorter hair, is kneeling next to the first one, who is still on all fours and bleeding from the nose. The second assassin is running up to them.

  1. ASSASSIN 3: You still kicking, Ches?
  2. ASSASSIN 1: I dink dat &@$#@!% broke by dose.
  3. ASSASSIN 2: What?
  4. ASSASSIN 1: Nebber bine, Buddy.

Panel Three
Angle from directly above Batman, still pulling Gorman up the stairs.

  1. BATMAN: Are there any other samples of Panacea in the building?
  2. GORMAN: The safe in my office – why?
  3. BATMAN: So I can protect you and them at the same time.

Panel Four
Batman is pushing a door open onto the next floor.

  1. BATMAN: Oracle. IDs on the assassins. Three women, two with knives, one with guns.
  2. ORACLE: [Comm] Asian and two blondes?
  3. BATMAN: Check.
  4. ORACLE: [Comm] Sounds like the Ravens. Leader is Cheshire. Bad news. Poison expert. Pistolera and Vicious are above-average in combat but not that bright. Names are pretty indicative.
  5. GORMAN: Who are you talking to? Where are we going? My office isn’t on this floor.

Panel Five
Batman is prying open the doors of the adjacent elevator.

  1. BATMAN: None of your concern, up, and we’re taking the elevator.
  2. GORMAN: Won’t those crazy people be inside?!

Panel Six
Shot from inside the elevator shaft of Batman pulling out his grapnel gun.

  1. BATMAN: You ask too many questions.

PAGE SEVENTEEN – ONE PANEL

Panel One
Shot from above Batman and Gorman. Batman is grappling up through the elevator shaft with Gorman in his free arm.

  1. GORMAN: AAAAAAAAHHHHHHHH!

PAGE EIGHTEEN – TWO PANELS

Panel One
The Ravens are standing in the elevator, PISTOLERA on the left, VICIOUS on the right, and CHESHIRE in the middle. Vicious is tapping her fingers on the wall.

  1. SFX: T-t-t-t-tap!

Panel Two
The same shot. Pistolera looks at Vicious. Cheshire is grimacing.

  1. SFX: T-t-t-t-tap!
  2. PISTOLERA: You know, we could’ve taken the stai-
  3. VICIOUS: Shut up, Bunny.

PAGE NINETEEN – FOUR PANELS

Panel One
The Ravens burst out of the elevator into a carpeted hallway, brandishing their weapons.

Panel Two
Closeup of Vicious.

  1. VICIOUS: Nobody here?

Panel Three
Closeup of Pistolera.

  1. PISTOLERA: Maybe not here yet?

Panel Four
Closeup of Cheshire.

  1. CHESHIRE: Mebbe ulreddy here. Buddy, jeck leff. Vish, wash de doors. I jeck right.
  2. PISTOLERA: [Off-panel] Got it. I… think.

PAGE TWENTY – SIX PANELS

Panel One
Batman is crouched behind a corner. Cheshire is approaching towards the reader on the right. Gorman is cowering behind Batman to the reader’s left. On the far right is a boardroom separated from the hallway by a glass wall.

  1. BATMAN: [Cap] Don’t know what weapons she has poisoned. Can’t risk much contact.

Panel Two
Close in and turn the angle a little to the right so we see more of the boardroom. Batman lunges forward and grabs Cheshire by the arm.

Panel Three
Shift so we’re a full ninety degrees from Panel One’s angle, closing in over Batman’s shoulder. He’s throwing Cheshire through the glass wall into the boardroom.

  1. SFX: KRRRRSSSSH!

Panel Four
Closeup on Cheshire. She’s picked up a glass shard from the wall and is spitting on it.

  1. BATMAN: [Cap] Even her spit’s poisoned. This isn’t a fight I can afford to have with a civilian in tow.

Panel Five
Batman throws down a smoke pellet and begins to turn, pushing Gorman ahead of him down the hallway.

Panel Six
The smoke begins to fill the hall as the poisoned glass shard flies into the cloud.

  1. ORACLE: [Comm] Batman, I just noticed something.

PAGE TWENTY-ONE – THREE PANELS

Panel One
Low angle of Batman pulling Gorman down a hallway.

  1. BATMAN: I’m a little busy right now, Oracle.
  2. ORACLE: [Comm] This is important.

Panel Two
High angle of the same hallway, empty. The visual is grainy, as through the view of a low-quality camera.

  1. ORACLE: [Comm] The security cameras aren’t showing you in the halls.

Panel Three
Batman and Gorman have reached a door with a plaque next to it saying “GUS GORMAN – CEO.” Batman is turning the doorknob.

  1. ORACLE: [Comm] That means someone’s looping the footage, which means—

PAGE TWENTY-TWO – ONE PANEL

Panel One
Batman has pushed open the office door, revealing a slick but unadorned office. Several cardboard boxes are still stacked in the corner. Standing in front of the safe in the back wall is none other than CATWOMAN.

  1. ORACLE: [Comm] —Someone else is in the building.
  2. CATWOMAN: … Oops.
  3. CAPTION: To be continued! Be here in thirty!
